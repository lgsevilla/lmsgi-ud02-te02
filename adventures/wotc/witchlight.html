Sample Encounter: The Trickster’s Ticket

The party arrives at the Witchlight Carnival to find a commotion at the ticket booth. A pair of goblin twins, Pim and Pom, have been accused of stealing enchanted tickets that allow entry to the “Hall of Reflections.” Each insists the other is guilty. A harried pixie attendant begs the adventurers to sort things out before the Witchlight Monarch’s parade begins.

Goals

  • Encourage the players to interact socially instead of fighting.
  • Introduce the carnival’s whimsical tone and Feywild-style logic.
  • Provide a moral choice: fairness versus opportunity.

Encounter Details

Location
The ticket booth near the carnival gate
Recommended Level
1–2
Type
Social / Investigation
Creatures
2 Goblin Tricksters (non-lethal)
Skill Focus
Insight, Persuasion, Sleight of Hand
Outcome
Players who uncover the truth earn the goblins’ trust and gain a shortcut to the Hall of Reflections. If they fail, they are caught in a harmless illusionary maze that delays them for 10 minutes of carnival time.

GM Tip: This encounter is best used early to establish that cleverness and empathy are as valuable as strength in the Witchlight Carnival.

Key Locations & Moments

The Witchlight Carnival

Theme · Wonder bound by rules

The carnival is both gateway and test — a place of joy, regret, and half-truths. Track time carefully; every event is an opportunity for magic or mischief.

Crossing into the Feywild

Theme · Choices and consequences

Players who pass the carnival’s test step into the Feywild realm of Prismeer. Every choice echoes — even small acts of kindness or greed ripple outward.
